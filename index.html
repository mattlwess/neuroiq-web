The neuroscience

Your brain wasn't designed to stop learning at bedtime.

During NREM sleep, the hippocampus replays memories and transfers them to the neocortex for long-term storage. Sleep spindles — the exact waveform in our logo — are the EEG signature of this process happening in real time.

NeuroIQ's inter-stimulus intervals are precisely calibrated to these natural encoding windows. Data delivers. Spindle fires. You encode. Repeat — hundreds of times a night.

Sleep spindles (12–15 Hz) are the brain's memory consolidation signature. They appear in NREM Stage 2 and directly correlate with next-day recall.

Inter-stimulus intervals (ISI) — the deliberate pauses between data packets — allow the hippocampus to process each unit before the next arrives.

Neuroplasticity during sleep is when the brain physically forms new synaptic connections. NeuroIQ targets this window with user-defined content.
